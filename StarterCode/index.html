Income and Obesity

Does Income Have an Effect on Obesity Rates?

Income vs. Obesity

When looking at the graph, a general trend can be seen. Many of the states that have lower incomes also have higher obesity rates.

There are several reasons that this trend could be occurring. First, food that is inexpensive to buy typically is not as healthy for those with lower incomes. Inexpensive food options usually have lower nutritional value than foods like lean protein, fruits and vegetables. Many people on a fixed income do not buy perishable items because they go bad faster.

Another reason is accessibility. It would be interesting to look at the number of grocery stores in each state. If there is a "food desert" situation, many people eat food that is convenient to get from smaller, non-grocery stores. Most convenience stores have food that is lower in nutritional value because that is what sells better.
